Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I should pursue litigation?

Litigation is a significant commitment of time and resources. Before recommending it, we assess the strength of your claim or defence, the likely cost, and whether alternative resolution methods — negotiation or mediation — might achieve a better outcome faster. We'll give you an honest assessment at the outset.

Which courts do you appear in?

We appear in the Magistrates' Court, Sessions Court, and High Court. For matters that require representation in the Court of Appeal or Federal Court, we work with senior counsel where appropriate.

How long does litigation typically take in Malaysia?

Timelines vary significantly depending on the complexity of the matter and court availability. A straightforward Magistrates' Court claim may resolve within 6–12 months; High Court matters often take 2–4 years. We will give you a realistic expectation once we understand your matter.

Can you help me recover a debt without going to court?

In many cases, yes. A formal letter of demand from a solicitor is often sufficient to prompt settlement without filing. Where court proceedings are necessary, we can apply for summary judgment in straightforward debt recovery cases, which can significantly shorten the process.
